Outside of Work

Outside of engineering, I enjoy sports and traveling.

The things that slow life down and require long-term dedication and presence have deeply shaped the way I approach life, work, and the world around me.

Yoga Practice

Mindfulness & Teaching

I’ve practiced yoga for over seven years. With a desire to share what I’ve learned, I also became a certified yoga instructor and have been teaching consistently for the past two years.

Yoga has helped me find balance between work and life. It has also taught me to become more focused, patient, and approach people and situations with greater softness and understanding.

My interest in mindfulness also led me to join a yoga and Ayurveda program in India, where I explored meditation, sound healing, and a slower pace of living.

Endurance in Nature

Endurance & Exploration

I enjoy outdoor activities that require endurance and presence, including diving, hiking, mountaineering, and long-distance running. I also enjoy the process of gradually building strength and experiencing the small progress my body makes over time.

I’ve traveled to different places for diving and hiking, walked part of the Camino de Santiago in Spain, and completed a full marathon.

At the same time, I also enjoy solo travel. It allows me to step away from familiar routines and perspectives, slow down in unfamiliar environments, spend time with myself, reflect, and observe the world more openly.

These experiences remind me to stay calm, move steadily, and appreciate consistent progress rather than simply chasing speed.
